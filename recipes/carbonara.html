Spaghetti alla carbonara

Description

A carbonara made with pasta that has been boiled into full submission, coated in a warm scrambled egg situation, studded with microwaved meat and finished with a bold ketchup drizzle for what can only be described as acidity. Seasoned aggressively with every pepper in a three-household radius and topped with a single ceremonial pea, it is a dish that defies both Italian law and basic physics, best served to someone who loves you unconditionally.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti (cooked until it has given up)
  • 3 eggs (scrambled, technically by accident)
  • Some cheese (any cheese, including emotional cheese)
  • Pancetta (microwaved)
  • Pepper (all of it)
  • Ketchup (for acidity)
  • 1 pea

Steps

  1. Collect Thursday water before noon and bring to a boil over medium existential dread
  2. Cook spaghetti for 47 minutes until fully surrendered, then drain and leave in the sink to air out
  3. Whisk eggs, cheese, and mild optimism together until the mixture is a color
  4. Add all the pepper. Then more pepper. Then your neighbor's pepper.
  5. Place pancetta in a cold pan and wait 40 minutes. Nothing will happen. Microwave it for 11 seconds.
  6. Dump the egg mixture onto screaming hot pasta and stir too slowly
  7. Observe the scrambling. Accept it.
  8. Transfer to a white plate with quiet dignity
  9. Drizzle ketchup generously for acidity
  10. Place one (1) pea on top
  11. Add a basil sprig so it looks like you tried
  12. Serve immediately and leave the room
